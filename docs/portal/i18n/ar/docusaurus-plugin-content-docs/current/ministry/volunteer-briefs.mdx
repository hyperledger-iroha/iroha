## Overview

Volunteer briefs capture the support/oppose/context viewpoints that the Ministry of Information reviews whenever blacklist or policy changes are proposed. Every submission must follow the canonical schema implemented by `iroha_data_model::ministry::VolunteerBriefV1` (version `1`) so that:

1. Governance dashboards can compare fact tables deterministically.
2. Conflict-of-interest disclosures are enforced before publication.
3. Off-topic or astroturfed submissions are suppressed without removing them from the audit trail.

Use the template in `docs/source/ministry/volunteer_brief_template.md` or the `cargo xtask ministry-transparency ingest` helper to build a payload. The CLI validates the Norito struct, lints moderation tags, and emits a summary showing how many fact rows, citations, and disclosures were accepted.

## Publication pipeline & SLA

| Stage | Window | Details |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Intake & validation | T+0 to 6 hours | Payloads are uploaded through the volunteer form or the CLI. `VolunteerBriefV1::validate` enforces the schema (version, RFC 3339 timestamp, disclosure policy, digest formatting). Failures are rejected immediately with actionable error codes. |
| Translation & moderation | T+6 to 24 hours | Reviewers apply moderation tags (`needs-translation`, `duplicate`, `policy-escalation`, etc.), redact contacts, and flag off-topic entries. Tags are stored in the Norito payload so downstream automation can suppress or highlight entries. |
| Portal publication | T+24 to 48 hours | Valid briefs are published alongside the corresponding proposal page under **Volunteer Opinions**. Off-topic entries remain in the raw archive but are excluded from aggregate stance counts. |

Expedited handling (≤12 hours) is triggered automatically when the `policy-escalation` or `astroturf` tags are present. Operators can audit compliance via the Volunteer Briefs Grafana panel, which tracks the time between `submitted_at` and portal publication.

## Deliverables

- **Schema & docs:** `VolunteerBriefV1` lives in the shared data model, and the detailed field guide plus example payload reside in `docs/source/ministry/volunteer_brief_template.md` and `docs/examples/ministry/volunteer_brief_template.json`.
- **Validation tooling:** `cargo xtask ministry-transparency volunteer-validate --input <file> [--json-output artifacts/ministry/volunteer-lint.json]` runs the same lints used by the portal build; `ingest --volunteer <file>` remains available when you want to stage an entire bundle for publication.
- **Portal surfacing:** Each proposal page links to the decoded support/oppose/context briefs, making citizen opinions visible without downloading raw JSON. Moderation metadata is rendered alongside the brief so jurors understand why an entry may have been delayed or excluded.

Refer to the roadmap item **MINFO-3 — Volunteer interest panel infrastructure** if additional automation (e.g., bulk import APIs or extended analytics) is required.
