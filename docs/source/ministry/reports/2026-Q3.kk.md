# Executive Summary

The Ministry completed the first full transparency publish under the MINFO-8
plan. Hash and contextual moderation models stayed inside the ±3 percentage
point drift band while the vision policy was suppressed because of the low
sample count and is now queued for retraining. Appeals landed at 412 cases,
with 98.3 % resolved inside the SLA and a 41.6 hour median turnaround.
Denylist churn netted +76 entries (four emergency quarantines) and treasury
flows left **53 150 XOR** reserved for upcoming dispute budgets. All artefacts
were sanitized with ε counts = 0.75 / ε accuracy = 0.5, anchored in
`artifacts/ministry/transparency/2026-Q3/`, and mirrored to SoraFS CID
`7f4c2d81a6b13579ccddeeff00112233`.

## Quarter in Review

### Highlights
- Hash + contextual models remained within the ±3 pp false-positive/false-negative guardrail; the vision policy was suppressed after only 52 samples and has a dedicated retraining run scheduled.
- Appeals: 412 filed, 397 resolved, 98.3 % inside SLA with seven breaches escalated to the governance council; 18 cases reopened for additional evidence.
- Treasury: 148 750 XOR deposits vs 95 600 XOR payouts, keeping **53 150 XOR** earmarked for Q4 dispute reserves while volunteer coverage reached 128 briefs (91.5 % citation coverage across EN/JA/ES).
