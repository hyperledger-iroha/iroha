# SoraNet GAR Attestation — United States (2027-Q2)

- **Review date:** 2027-03-18
- **Reviewed by:** SoraNet Legal & Compliance Desk (Privacy + Telecom working group)
- **Decision:** Direct-only transport required (no anonymous SoraNet circuits) for production traffic originating from or terminating in the United States.
- **Rationale:** US discovery obligations and data-residency carve-outs require operator-verifiable hops and deterministic audit trails; anonymous relays would prevent regulators from confirming jurisdictional handling or executing lawful intercept orders.
- **Scope:** Applies to SoraFS gateway fetch/upload traffic, relay incentives, DA ingestion/fetches, and Torii `/v1/stream` backhaul when any party is US-based. Cross-border traffic involving US endpoints inherits the direct-only rule.
- **Conditions/expiry:** Review again no later than 2027-09-30 or sooner if the legal position on encrypted relay chains changes.
- **Operational requirements:**
  - Publish the attestation digest in `compliance.attestations` under `jurisdiction: "US"` and ensure `jurisdiction_opt_outs` includes `US`.
  - Operators must keep the signed PDF memo (`us-2027-q2.pdf`) in their governance archive; the Blake2b-256 digest of this Markdown companion is used for quick verification in automation.
  - Capture activation evidence (git tag, rollout window, and operator sign-off) in the local GAR logbook.
- **Contacts:** `governance@soranet.org`, `legal@soranet.org`

The canonical digest for this attestation (Blake2b-256, uppercase hex) is recorded in the jurisdictional review log.
