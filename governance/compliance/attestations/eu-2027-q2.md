# SoraNet GAR Attestation — EU/EEA (2027-Q2)

- **Review date:** 2027-03-18
- **Reviewed by:** SoraNet Legal & Compliance Desk (Privacy + Telecom working group)
- **Decision:** Anonymous SoraNet transport permitted provided GAR/policy telemetry remains enabled and DP/privacy safeguards stay within the published budgets. Direct-only fallback remains available for regulated tenants that opt out explicitly.
- **Rationale:** Current GDPR guidance allows encrypted relay chains when data minimisation, observability, and audit proofs are in place. The SNNet-8 privacy pipeline (Prio shares + suppression budgets) and GAR enforcement receipts meet the documented obligations for the reviewed services.
- **Scope:** Applies to SoraFS gateway traffic, Torii streaming, DA ingestion/fetch, and relay incentives across EU/EEA endpoints. Operators must disable direct-only overrides unless local regulation mandates them.
- **Conditions/expiry:** Re-assess by 2027-09-30 or sooner if the privacy budget or DP calibration changes materially. Any change to the SNNet-8 suppression budget requires a new attestation.
- **Operational requirements:**
  - Keep `jurisdiction_opt_outs` empty for EU/EEA unless a tenant files an explicit opt-out using the GAR template.
  - Store the signed memo (`eu-2027-q2.pdf`) in the governance archive; the Blake2b-256 digest of this Markdown companion is used for automation checks.
  - Ensure privacy dashboards and suppression reports are linked in the GAR logbook for each activation.
- **Contacts:** `governance@soranet.org`, `legal@soranet.org`, `dpo@soranet.org`

The canonical digest for this attestation (Blake2b-256, uppercase hex) is recorded in the jurisdictional review log.
